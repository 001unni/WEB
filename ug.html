Undergraduate Courses

  • Computer Science

    A comprehensive program covering programming, data structures, algorithms, and software development.

  • Mathematics

    Focus on pure and applied mathematics, including calculus, algebra, and statistical methods.

  • Physics

    Covers classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum physics, and modern physics concepts.

  • Chemistry

    Includes organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry, with practical lab experience.

  • Biology

    Study of living organisms, including genetics, ecology, microbiology, and human biology.

  • Economics

    Analyzes economic theories, microeconomics, macroeconomics, and economic policies.

  • B.Com (Bachelor of Commerce)

    Focuses on business, finance, and accounting principles, preparing students for careers in commerce and industry.

  • English Literature

    Explores literary works, theories, and criticism from various historical periods and genres.

  • History

    Studies human history from ancient times to the present, including political, social, and cultural developments.

  • Political Science

    Covers government systems, political theories, and international relations, examining political behavior and institutions.

  • Psychology

    Investigates human behavior, mental processes, and emotional well-being, covering various psychological theories and practices.

  • Languages (French, Spanish, German, etc.)

    Provides proficiency in foreign languages along with an understanding of cultural and literary contexts.

  • Fine Arts

    Encourages creative expression through visual arts, including painting, sculpture, and digital art.

  • Sociology

    Examines social structures, relationships, and institutions, exploring issues such as inequality, culture, and social change.
